Gambling Game

You will play a dice gambling game with 10 trials. You start the game with 300 points. During each trial you have the chance to earn more. Each trial consists of 3 stages.

The Goal: At the end of each trial you win if your total dice sum is higher than the House's total sum.

The Setup: At the start of a trial, both you and the House start with a sum of 0.

The Process:

At the beginning of each trial you bet 15 points to play. Then the three stages follow.

At the beginning of each stage you have three options:

  • Add: Increase your bet by 5 points.
  • Hold: Keep your bet the same.
  • Retract: Decrease your bet by 5 points.

After you have made your choice you and the house each roll a dice. The numbers you roll are added to your respective totals. If you have a higher dice sum at the end of the trial you get paid twice your bet. If the house has a higher or equal dice sum you lose the betting amount.

Accuracy Score

Unlike a casino, we don't just pay you for winning. We pay you for knowing whether you will win.

After every betting decision, we will ask: "What is the probability that you will win this round?" (0% to 100%).

We calculate an Accuracy Score for every prediction you make.

How it works:

  • If you say 100% and you win, you get a Perfect Score (1.0).
  • If you say 100% and you lose, you get a Zero Score (0.0).
  • If you say 50%, you get a safe, middle-of-the-road score regardless of the outcome.

The Trap: This scoring system penalizes overconfidence. Being "sure" and being wrong hurts your accuracy score more than if you're wrong and uncertain.

The Strategy: The math is designed so that the only way to get the highest possible score is to report exactly what you truly believe.

Your Payout

You are guaranteed $3 for completing this experiment, but you will also get a bonus depending on how well you do in the game. Your bonus depends on two numbers:

  • Your Wealth: The points you have accumulated from the dice game (Potential Earnings).
  • Your Multiplier: Your average Accuracy Score across the entire experiment.

Cash Payout = Base Pay + (Points x Accuracy Score) / 100

Examples:

  • You win big (600 points) but guess randomly (50% accuracy): You take home (600 * 0.5)/100 = $3 bonus + $3 base pay for $6 in total.
  • You win big (600 points) and predict your wins/losses well (90% accuracy): You take home (600 * 0.9)/100 = $5.40 bonus + $3 base pay for $8.40 in total.
  • You win big (600 points) but lie about your confidence (saying 100% when it was risky), resulting in 10% accuracy: You take home (600 * 0.1)/100 = $0.60 bonus + $3 base pay for $3.60 in total.

Bottom Line: To make the most money, play smart to build your bank, but be brutally honest about your chances.
